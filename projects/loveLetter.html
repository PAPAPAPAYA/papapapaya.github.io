Love Letter Visual Redesign

2019 /  Art Direction Class /  Medibang /  Collaborative project with Xuwen Yi 许文逸  /  Card design for the board game Love Letter

A different visual take on the card game Love Letter, done for Art Direction class.

Constructivism was chosen (thanks to Wenshun Tang) to encourage a different play style by emphasizing the class conflict between cards.


I - Soldier

Wenyi first did a sketch of a solder. The figure is looking up to indicate it being lower in the hierarchy.

I then cleaned it up and decreased the amount of details to make it more graphical and visually clear. Also I stack 5 of them together since it is an important information that should be on the card to indicate the amount of this card in the deck. I arranged them at the bottom lest and directed to the upper right to suggest their lower class in the game.

Finally, text and more graphic elements to enhence the effect.


II - Wizard

Wenyi did the original skecth.

This time, there are 2 wizards in the deck, and they are at the middle of the hierarchy, so I arranged them in the middle and went for a more balanced composition.

Finally, radial elements were introduced to give a sense of magic, which is corresponded with the effect and the subject of the card.


III - Princess

Like the others, Wenyi did this sketch. This time, the figure is looking down.

There is only 1 princess in the deck and it is the most powerful and decisive card, so it is put in the center. Contour was adjusted to form a more triangular composition, makes the figure appears higher up, more commanding and with confident.

Text were arranged in a way to enhence that downward energy, while stripes of color were used to convey its great power, emphasizing the figure in the middle.


Comparison with Original Design

Information from the original cards were mostly preserved, except in the original cards, there is an effect icon which represents the card's effect right below the number at the top left. Since it didn't effect the playability, it was omitted in my design.

The original design is more of a fairly tale theme, I believe the redesign successfully changed the feeling of the cards dramatically and would definitely encourage a very different feeling of play.
